— Here's what I've done so far —

Taipei American School

Class of 2006
Taipei, Taiwan

This is home to my upbringing. From learning a third langugage in Japanese, to editing films, to making 3D animations, to learning to play the violin, I had the privilege to explore my curiosities at an early age. Here I was captain of the varsity soccer and badminton teams, through which I first learned to be a team player and a leader. Having attended an international school with such strong ethnic diversity, I learned not only to respect diversity, but also to value it. As a result, I became close friends with many international kids, some of which are still my closests friends today.

University of Southern California

Viterbi School of Engineering, BS & MS in Electrical Engineering, BS in 2010, MS in 2011
Los Angeles, CA

Similar to my high school, USC also had a diverse crowd, making my transition to college rather smooth. Having had a passion for tech and music, I took music courses and as many audio-related engineering courses as I could and built many audio-related engineering projects. Aside from all the classes that I had taken at USC, perhaps the most valuable lesson came from working with my fellow classmates. It taught me to be a better problem solver. Outside of academia, my two favorite activities were playing basketall and attending college football games. I really enjoyed the social life in college.

Intel Corporation

Software Engineer, 2011-2013
Santa Clara, CA

My first full-time job out of school was a software engineering position at Intel in the Bay Area. Working at Intel really helped me mature in so many aspects. For one, working with so many brilliant engineers taught me to work more efficiently.
